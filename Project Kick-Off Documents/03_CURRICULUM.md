# SaxStart — Lesson Curriculum

## Structure

4 modules, 17 lessons total for MVP launch.  
Each lesson has: title, duration, objective, steps, fingering reference, tip.

---

## Module 1: Foundations
**Goal:** Get the instrument ready and make your first sound.  
**Lessons:** 4  
**Unlocked by default**

---

### Lesson 1.1 — Meet Your Saxophone
**Duration:** 2 min  
**Objective:** Learn the parts of the alto saxophone.

**Steps:**
1. Look at the saxophone laid out on a table
2. Identify: bell, body, bow, neck, mouthpiece
3. Identify: keys, octave key, thumb rest
4. Learn the reed: what it does, how fragile it is

**Tip:** Never put the saxophone down with the mouthpiece facing down. Always lay it in the case.

---

### Lesson 1.2 — How to Hold It
**Duration:** 3 min  
**Objective:** Sit correctly with the saxophone supported by the strap.

**Steps:**
1. Attach the neck strap — adjust so the mouthpiece reaches your mouth naturally
2. Sit straight, feet flat on floor
3. Sax hangs on your right side, slightly in front
4. Right thumb under thumb rest, left thumb on octave key
5. Do not squeeze — let the strap carry the weight

**Tip:** If your neck hurts after 5 minutes, your strap is too short or too long. Adjust before playing.

---

### Lesson 1.3 — Embouchure Basics
**Duration:** 3 min  
**Objective:** Form the correct mouth shape for saxophone.

**Steps:**
1. Say the word "WEE" and hold that jaw shape
2. Top teeth rest gently on top of the mouthpiece
3. Lips wrap around the mouthpiece — no biting, no puffing cheeks
4. Corners of mouth pulled slightly inward (like saying "mmm")
5. Practice in front of a mirror if possible

**Tip:** A tight bite will produce a squeaky, thin sound. A relaxed but firm jaw gives a full tone.

---

### Lesson 1.4 — Breathing Basics
**Duration:** 3 min  
**Objective:** Use diaphragm breathing for saxophone tone.

**Steps:**
1. Place one hand on your belly
2. Breathe in through the corners of your mouth — belly should push OUT
3. Exhale slowly and steadily — imagine fogging a window
4. Practice 5 full breath cycles before playing
5. When you play, the air should feel like you're exhaling on that foggy window

**Tip:** Shallow chest breathing makes saxophone sound thin and unstable. The air comes from the belly.

---

## Module 2: First Notes
**Goal:** Play your first three notes and switch between them.  
**Lessons:** 5  
**Requires:** Module 1 complete

---

### Lesson 2.1 — Making Your First Sound
**Duration:** 3 min  
**Objective:** Produce any sound from the saxophone.

**Steps:**
1. Set embouchure (Lesson 1.3)
2. Take a full belly breath
3. Put tongue behind top teeth like saying "tah"
4. Blow and release tongue at the same time — "tah"
5. Don't worry about the note yet — just make a sound!

**Tip:** If you get a squeak, you're either biting too hard or not enough air. Relax and blow more.

---

### Lesson 2.2 — Note B
**Duration:** 3 min  
**Objective:** Play Note B clearly and hold it for 4 beats.

**Fingering:** Left hand only — press the top key (index finger only)  
All other keys up.

**Steps:**
1. Set posture and embouchure
2. Press only the top left key (index finger)
3. Take a full breath
4. Say "tah" into the mouthpiece and hold the air for 4 beats
5. Rest 2 beats. Repeat 5 times.

**Tip:** Keep your jaw steady — do not move it while holding the note. Let the air be the engine.

---

### Lesson 2.3 — Note A
**Duration:** 3 min  
**Objective:** Play Note A clearly and hold it for 4 beats.

**Fingering:** Left hand — press top two keys (index + middle finger)  
All other keys up.

**Steps:**
1. From B fingering, add your middle finger
2. Make sure both fingers sit in the center of the keys
3. Play and hold for 4 beats
4. Rest and repeat 5 times

**Tip:** Note A is lower than B. You may need slightly more air to keep it stable.

---

### Lesson 2.4 — Note G
**Duration:** 3 min  
**Objective:** Play Note G clearly and hold it for 4 beats.

**Fingering:** Left hand — press top three keys (index + middle + ring finger)  
All other keys up.

**Steps:**
1. From A fingering, add your ring finger
2. All three fingers should feel relaxed — curved, not flat
3. Play and hold for 4 beats
4. Rest and repeat 5 times

**Tip:** G is the lowest of the three. Slow, steady air gives the best G tone.

---

### Lesson 2.5 — Switching Between B, A, G
**Duration:** 5 min  
**Objective:** Smoothly move between B, A, and G in sequence.

**Fingering:** Alternating between B (1 key), A (2 keys), G (3 keys)

**Steps:**
1. Play B for 2 beats, then A for 2 beats — repeat 4 times
2. Play A for 2 beats, then G for 2 beats — repeat 4 times
3. Play B → A → G, 2 beats each — repeat 4 times
4. Try going faster: 1 beat each note

**Tip:** Keep fingers close to the keys. Don't lift them too high — it slows you down.

## Module 3: Tone Building
**Goal:** Develop a cleaner, fuller, more stable sound.  
**Lessons:** 4  
**Requires:** Module 2 complete

---

### Lesson 3.1 — Long Tones
**Duration:** 5 min  
**Objective:** Hold each note for 8 full beats with a steady tone.

**Steps:**
1. Play B — hold for 8 slow beats (count in your head)
2. Rest 4 beats
3. Play A — hold for 8 beats
4. Rest 4 beats
5. Play G — hold for 8 beats

**Tip:** Long tones are the most important exercise for saxophone. Do them every single day.

---

### Lesson 3.2 — Air Support
**Duration:** 4 min  
**Objective:** Use consistent air pressure to keep tone stable.

**Steps:**
1. Take the deepest breath you can
2. Play G and imagine pushing air from the belly, not the throat
3. Hold for 8 beats — the volume should stay even throughout
4. If it gets quieter, you're running out of belly support

**Tip:** Your ribs should stay expanded for the full note. Collapse = weaker air = thinner tone.

---

### Lesson 3.3 — Tonguing Basics
**Duration:** 4 min  
**Objective:** Start each note cleanly using the tongue.

**Steps:**
1. With mouthpiece in mouth, say "tah" to start the air
2. Between notes, tongue gently blocks the reed to stop sound
3. Play: B — tah (stop) — tah (stop) — tah
4. Keep the air column moving even between notes
5. Tongue should be light — not a hard "t" attack

**Tip:** Think "dah" instead of "tah" for a softer, more musical start.

---

### Lesson 3.4 — Tone Stability
**Duration:** 5 min  
**Objective:** Play long tones without wobble or drift.

**Steps:**
1. Record yourself (phone voice memo) playing a long B
2. Listen back — is the pitch drifting? Is it airy?
3. Play again and compare
4. Try using Play Mode in the app to score your stability

**Tip:** A slight wobble can be natural, but pitch drift means air is uneven. Think of a laser beam, not a river.

## Module 4: First Music
**Goal:** Play simple rhythms and your first melody.  
**Lessons:** 4  
**Requires:** Module 3 complete

---

### Lesson 4.1 — Rhythm Basics
**Duration:** 4 min  
**Objective:** Understand quarter notes, half notes, whole notes.

**Steps:**
1. Clap: quarter note = 1 beat, half = 2 beats, whole = 4 beats
2. With metronome at 60 BPM, clap the pattern: Q Q Q Q (4 quarter notes)
3. Now clap: H H (2 half notes)
4. Now: W (1 whole note)
5. Mix: Q Q H (counts 1-2-3-4)

**Tip:** Set the metronome first. Never practice rhythm without a pulse.

---

### Lesson 4.2 — Simple Patterns
**Duration:** 5 min  
**Objective:** Play rhythmic patterns on B, A, G.

**Steps:**
1. Metronome at 60 BPM
2. Play: B B B B (quarter notes)
3. Play: G G A A B B B (quarter notes with hold on last B)
4. Rest, then repeat
5. Increase to 70 BPM when comfortable

**Tip:** If you make a mistake, don't stop. Keep the rhythm going and fix it next time through.

---

### Lesson 4.3 — First Melody
**Duration:** 5 min  
**Objective:** Play a simple 8-bar melody using B, A, G.

**Melody (Mary Had a Little Lamb — B A G style):**
```
B  A  G  A  |  B  B  B  -  |
A  A  A  -  |  B  B  B  -  |
B  A  G  A  |  B  B  B  B  |
A  A  B  A  |  G  -  -  -  |
```
(Each cell = 1 beat, `-` = hold)

**Steps:**
1. Say the note names out loud in rhythm first
2. Then finger the notes without blowing
3. Then play slowly with metronome at 50 BPM
4. Increase speed when confident

**Tip:** This melody only uses 3 notes but sounds like music. That's the point — music is not about how many notes you know.

---

### Lesson 4.4 — Beginner Scale Intro
**Duration:** 5 min  
**Objective:** Play a simple partial C major scale.

**Notes used:** G, A, B, C, D  
*(C and D fingerings introduced here)*

**Steps:**
1. Learn C fingering: left middle + right index + right middle
2. Learn D fingering: no keys (open)
3. Play slowly: G → A → B → C → D
4. Then back down: D → C → B → A → G
5. Metronome at 50 BPM, one note per beat

**Tip:** You now know 5 notes. That's enough to play hundreds of songs. Keep going.
